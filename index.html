THE PROBLEM OF TECHNOLOGICAL WASTE IN THE WORLD.

The volume of e-waste produced in the world and poor recycling management endanger the environment. Some of the most common substances found in these discarded items include cadmium, lead, lead oxide, antimony, nickel, and mercury. These toxic elements pollute rivers, lakes and seas and release gases into the atmosphere that disrupt ecosystems. Therefore, the return to the production and consumption model that reduces the amount of e-waste cannot be put off any longer.

Responsible consumption, capable of lengthening useful life and slowing the growth of this refuse is one of the answers to the problem. "The reuse of technological devices has become the only option, in the face of an inefficient recycling system, to reduce the levels of electronic waste", points out Thibaud de Larauze, CEO of Back Market. In 2014 it was opted for electronic refurbishment and was considered to be one of the most innovative.

HOW CAN WE REDUCE TECHNOLOGICAL GARBAGE?

"Global e-waste is the fastest growing waste stream", asserts Peter Bakker, president of the World Business Council for Sustainable Development (WBCSD). Faced with this, he is pressing for urgent measures. Let's take a look at some of them:

Reduce.

We are using more devices and replacing them more frequently. Changing this habit is dependent on both the consumer — who should be less susceptible to consumption-inducing marketing strategies — and manufacturers, who are increasingly adopting policies such as ecodesign.

Reuse.

Experts in electronic recycling recommend that working devices be passed down to friends and family or sold on the secondary market. It is also possible to donate them to specialized charities.

Recycle.

Recycling should be an option when an item no longer works and there is no chance of it being used by someone close. The consumer has the choice of taking the old device to repair shops, e-waste collectors, or a company that specializes in electronic refurbishment.
